Making the Sauce


Ingredients
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • 200ml milk
  • Seasonings of choice (salt/white pepper), nutmeg, mustard
  • Optional: 100g cheese of your choice (grated)

    • Method
    1. Melt the butter in a small saucepan over low heat
    2. Add the flour and whisk until incorporated
    3. Cook the flour and butter out for a minute to prevent the end product tasting of raw flour
    4. Gradually add the milk as you whisk, the milk is added slowly so we can avoid lumps in the final sauce
    5. Once smooth you can add your seasonings. Salt, white pepper and nutmeg is traditionally used in a regular white sauce
    6. If adding cheese you can now add it to the sauce whilst constantly stirring to prevent it clumping up and burning at the bottom, you want to use a low heat so as to not split the cheese
    7. Adding cheese to the sauce makes it into a mornay sauce, I also like to add whole grain mustard and swap out the white pepper for black pepper

    The sauce is now ready, it should be just thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, the next recipe is for fresh pasta which we'll eventually turn into a lasagne.
